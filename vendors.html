Platform Scorecard

Five dimensions that drive total cost of ownership. Higher is always better for the buyer.

How these scores work — and what they aren't

Each score is a qualitative 0–10 judgment against a stated test, not a measurement. Higher = better for you, the buyer:

  • Cost Transparency — can you see real pricing without a sales call? 10 = full public pricing · 1 = quote-gated / enterprise-only.
  • Freedom from Lock-in — how hard is it to leave (proprietary formats, data export, contract length)? 10 = open formats, easy export · 1 = proprietary stack, long contracts.
  • SMB-Friendly — can a small team adopt it without a six-figure floor? 10 = free / low entry · 1 = enterprise minimums.
  • Freedom from Hidden Fees — egress, premium support, compute surprises? 10 = what you see is what you pay · 1 = many add-ons.
  • Support — quality and availability of help. 10 = strong enterprise support · low = community-only.

These are one practitioner's judgments from public pricing pages, G2 / Gartner Peer Insights reviews (2024–2025), and hands-on use — not audited, not vendor-confirmed, and not sponsored. Reasonable people will disagree on individual scores; that's expected. If you think one is wrong, open an issue with your reasoning and it changes in the open.

💡 Insider Tip: Use Open-Weight Models as Leverage

Even if you ultimately choose a proprietary platform, having a working prototype on HuggingFace / Llama gives you negotiating power — vendors price differently when they know you can walk. Enterprise software list prices generally carry healthy margin, so a quote is rarely the floor.

10 Questions to Ask Your AI Vendor Before Signing

Print this checklist and bring it to your next vendor meeting

  1. 1. What percentage of your quoted price covers data preparation and cleaning?

    If the answer is 0%, the quote is incomplete. Data prep is typically the largest single cost category (our model puts it around 30%).

  2. 2. What are the ongoing monthly costs after go-live?

    Monitoring, retraining, compute/inference, and support. Post-deployment costs exceed development costs within 12–18 months.

  3. 3. What is your production success rate for projects like ours?

    Rough rule of thumb: ~33% reach production for custom builds, ~55–65% for SaaS. Ask for references from similar-sized companies.

  4. 4. What happens to our data and models if we leave?

    Can you export in standard formats (ONNX, standard vector DBs)? Or are you locked into proprietary formats?

  5. 5. How do inference costs scale with usage?

    API pricing is often non-linear. A pilot at $3/day can become $45K/month at scale. Get the cost curve in writing.

  6. 6. What accuracy SLA do you commit to (not just uptime)?

    Most vendors guarantee uptime but not accuracy. If they won't commit to accuracy, that tells you something.

  7. 7. How do you handle model drift and retraining?

    Plan for quarterly retrains minimum. Each cycle costs $5K–$50K. Is this included in the quote?

  8. 8. What change management support do you provide?

    The #1 killer of AI projects isn't tech — it's people not using it. Budget 5–10% for training and adoption.

  9. 9. What's your realistic timeline from POC to production?

    Vendor promises average 2.5× optimistic. If they say 3 months, plan for 8.

  10. 10. Can I see your compliance documentation (SOC2, GDPR, AI Act)?

    If you're touching customer data, compliance costs $25K–$100K. Is this your cost or theirs?

Based on Gartner AI implementation guidelines, McKinsey 2024, NIST AI RMF, and practitioner interviews.
